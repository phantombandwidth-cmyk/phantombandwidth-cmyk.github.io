Privacy Policy

Effective date: March 20, 2026 · Applies to users in the United States, United Kingdom, and Australia

The short version: Folio never stores, syncs, or transmits your documents or annotations. There is no account system and no server. You can open documents from anywhere — your device, email, or a cloud service you choose — but once a file is open in Folio, it stays on your device. The only external transmission Folio initiates is a banner advertisement in the free version, and fonts loaded from Google on first launch.

What Folio Does Not Collect

Folio does not collect, transmit, or store any of the following:

  • The contents of any PDF file you open
  • Any annotations, highlights, notes, or signatures you create
  • Your name, email address, or any personally identifiable information
  • Your location
  • Usage patterns, session data, or feature interactions
  • Crash reports or diagnostic data of any kind

What Stays on Your Device

All data Folio generates is stored in your device's local app sandbox:

  • Annotations — stored in IndexedDB on your device; never transmitted anywhere
  • Document library index — stores file names and system URIs only, not file contents
  • App settings — theme, font, and preferences stored in localStorage
  • OCR text — processed entirely on-device using Google ML Kit; results are stored locally and never sent to Google or any third party

All locally stored data is permanently removed when you uninstall Folio, or immediately using Clear All Data in Settings.

Advertising (Free Version)

The free version of Folio displays a banner advertisement served by Google AdMob. AdMob may use a resettable advertising identifier on your device to serve relevant ads. This is separate from Folio's own data practices and is governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

You can permanently remove all advertising with the one-time Remove Ads in-app purchase in Settings. After purchase, no ads are requested or displayed. The AdMob SDK remains initialized but does not collect data or serve ads.

In-App Purchase

The Remove Ads purchase is processed entirely by Google Play. Folio does not receive or store your payment information. Your purchase entitlement is recorded on your device by Google Play Billing.

Third-Party Components

  • PDF.js (Mozilla Foundation) — runs entirely locally in a WebView; no data is sent anywhere
  • Apache PDFBox for Android — runs entirely locally; no data is sent anywhere
  • Google ML Kit Text Recognition — on-device OCR; no document content is sent to Google
  • Google AdMob — banner advertising in the free version (see Advertising section above)
  • Google Play Billing — processes the Remove Ads in-app purchase
  • Google Fonts — the fonts DM Sans and DM Mono are requested from Google's servers on first launch, after which they are cached locally. This means your device's IP address is transmitted to Google on first launch only.

Children's Privacy

Folio is not directed at children under 13. Folio does not knowingly collect personal information from children. The free version includes advertising served by AdMob, which is configured to serve non-personalized ads in compliance with applicable children's privacy laws.

Your Rights

Because Folio does not collect personal data about you, there is nothing for Folio to provide, correct, or delete on its end. Any data you want removed is stored locally on your device and can be deleted at any time using Clear All Data in Settings, or by uninstalling the app.

United Kingdom users: Folio's operation is consistent with the UK GDPR. Where AdMob processes data, Google acts as an independent data controller. You may direct data subject rights requests regarding Google's processing to Google directly.

Australian users: Folio's operation is consistent with the Australian Privacy Act 1988 and the Australian Privacy Principles. Contact details for privacy inquiries are in the Contact section below.

Availability

Folio is available on Google Play in the United States, United Kingdom, and Australia only.

Governing Law

This privacy policy is governed by the laws of the United States. For users in the United Kingdom, applicable UK law applies where required. For users in Australia, applicable Australian law applies where required.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes in a way that affects how your data is handled, the effective date above will be updated and the changes noted in Folio's release notes on Google Play.
